# Shikho MIS Analytics CEO Presentation Runbook

## Meeting Objective

This is not a dashboard walkthrough. This is a finance governance review.

The CEO needs answers to two questions:

1. Are we on track to deliver at least $500K full-year profit?
2. Do we have month-by-month cash visibility with downside scenario control?

Your answer:

- FY2026 outlook is positive, but not yet enough.
- FY2026 EBITDA outlook is about $297K.
- CEO target is $500K+.
- Gap is about $203K.
- Cash is manageable in Base case, but downside gets tight around July.
- Management needs decisions on revenue acceleration, S&M productivity, indirect cost control, and cash triggers.

## 90-Second Opening

Say this calmly:

> Today I will not walk through every chart. I will answer two questions: are we on track for $500K+ full-year profit, and do we have month-by-month cash visibility? The answer is: we are better than forecast through April, but not yet at the CEO profit target. The dashboard now shows exactly where the gap is and what management has to act on.

Then state:

- Reporting month: Apr-26
- Actuals through: Apr-26
- Forecast from: May-26
- Forecast to: Dec-26

Do not apologize. Do not explain the build history. Lead with control.

## Presentation Path

### 1. Executive Summary

Purpose: Give the answer before the evidence.

Dashboard action:

- Start on `Executive Summary`.
- Keep global period at `Apr-26`.
- Start in `Monthly`.
- Briefly switch to `YTD` only after monthly April weakness is clear.

Say:

- April EBITDA was weak: actual EBITDA was about -$110K.
- YTD is better than forecast, but full-year outlook is still below the $500K+ ambition.
- The rest of the review will explain the profit gap, the cost pressure, and the cash risk.

What to show:

- KPI cards only briefly.
- Revenue and EBITDA comparison.
- Gross Revenue to EBITDA bridge.
- Headline, risk, and action cards only if they support the profit/cash story.

What not to do:

- Do not explain every card.
- Do not spend time on design.
- Do not talk about old Budget. Say: "We have moved to actual vs forecast governance."

Transition:

> The first question is full-year profit. For that, the most important page is Forecast Outlook.

### 2. Forecast Outlook

Purpose: Answer whether the business is on track for $500K+ full-year profit.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `Forecast Outlook`.
- Point first to the FY KPI cards.
- Then show the Gross Revenue and EBITDA monthly trend.
- Then show the FY Gross Revenue to EBITDA bridge.
- Then show the forecast variance table.

Say:

- This is the main governance page.
- Jan-Apr is actual.
- May-Dec is forecast.
- FY Gross Revenue outlook is about $4.70M.
- FY Net Revenue outlook is about $4.49M.
- FY Gross Margin outlook is about $3.76M.
- FY EBITDA outlook is about $297K.
- Against a $500K+ target, the gap is about $203K.
- The gap is not a reporting issue. It is an operating target gap.

Interpretation:

- Revenue scale is material.
- Gross margin is strong at the full-year level.
- The main issue is below gross margin: S&M, brand marketing, indirect cost, payroll and operating cost discipline.
- The CEO conversation should move from "what happened" to "who owns the $203K bridge."

Decision ask:

- Assign one owner for the $203K profit gap bridge.
- Require a May-Dec profit recovery plan.
- Close the gap through revenue upside, S&M efficiency, and indirect cost control.

Transition:

> Profit is one side of governance. The other side is cash. A profitable plan is not enough if downside cash gets tight.

### 3. Cash & Runway

Purpose: Answer whether finance has month-by-month cash visibility and downside scenario control.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `Cash & Runway`.
- Start with the Base scenario.
- Show cash balance outlook.
- Then switch to Pessimistic.
- Highlight July.

Say:

- Base case is manageable.
- April closing cash was about $310K.
- Base case cash improves through June and July.
- But downside cash gets tight.
- Pessimistic July opening cash is about $5.5K.
- That means we need cash triggers before July, not after July.

What to show:

- Base scenario cash balance outlook.
- Pessimistic scenario cash balance outlook.
- Cash table rows for Apr-26, Jun-26, and Jul-26 if CEO asks for detail.

Decision ask:

- Approve a minimum cash threshold.
- Move to weekly cash trigger review before June.
- If downside revenue appears by June, freeze discretionary spend immediately.
- Separate P&L profit tracking from cash trigger tracking.

Transition:

> Now I will show where the profit is created and where it is consumed.

### 4. Actual vs Forecast P&L

Purpose: Explain where profit is created and where it is consumed.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `Actual vs Forecast Performance`.
- Keep period at `Apr-26`.
- Start in `Monthly`.
- Keep comparison selector on `Forecast`.
- Show the P&L table.
- Then switch to `YTD`.
- Use `Same Month Previous Year` only if CEO asks for YoY context.

Say:

- This is the control ledger.
- It shows actual vs forecast, not old budget.
- April EBITDA was negative because contribution was consumed below gross margin.
- YTD actual EBITDA is better than forecast, but the full year still needs a profit bridge.

Show in this exact order:

1. Gross Revenue
2. Net Revenue
3. Gross Margin
4. COGS
5. Academic Support
6. S&M Cost
7. Brand Marketing
8. Indirect Cost
9. EBITDA

How to handle questions:

- If CEO asks why gross margin moved, expand COGS.
- If CEO asks why EBITDA is weak, expand S&M and Indirect Cost.
- If CEO asks for trend, switch to YTD.
- If CEO asks if this is structural, go next to Cost & Margins.

Decision ask:

- Confirm which cost lines are allowed to scale with revenue.
- Confirm which cost lines require CEO approval before increasing.

Transition:

> The next question is whether revenue quality is strong enough to close the gap.

### 5. Revenue Analysis

Purpose: Explain whether revenue mix can support the profit target.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `Revenue Analysis`.
- Use `Monthly`.
- Show Channel Revenue.
- Show Course Revenue.
- Show New vs Repeat Revenue.
- Drill down only if CEO asks about the biggest movement.

Say:

- Revenue needs to be analyzed by source and repeatability.
- The CEO question is not only total revenue.
- The real question is which revenue streams can scale without cost rising at the same rate.
- Class 9-12 and Retention need special attention because they affect quality and repeatability.

Show:

- Channel mix: Telesales, Inbound, Installment Recovery, Retention.
- Course mix: Class 6-8, Class 9-12, Board Exam Prep, Uni Admission, Skills.
- New vs Repeat: whether growth is acquisition-led or repeat-led.

Decision ask:

- Which revenue stream can be pushed without proportional cost growth?
- Which channel deserves more capacity?
- Which channel should be constrained until productivity improves?

Transition:

> Revenue alone does not solve the target. We need to protect margin.

### 6. Cost & Margins

Purpose: Identify structural vs temporary cost pressure.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `Cost & Margins`.
- Use `Monthly` first.
- Show cash basis charts first.
- Use accrual charts only if explaining timing or run-rate.
- Click bars only for COGS, S&M, or Indirect Cost if the CEO asks.
- Switch to `YTD` after the monthly issue is understood.

Say:

- This page tells us whether cost growth is structural or temporary.
- Cash basis shows what hit the P&L/cash view.
- Accrual basis shows operational run-rate.
- We should not treat every cost the same.

Focus only on:

- COGS: teachers, streaming, database/server, book production.
- S&M: sales team and acquisition marketing.
- Indirect Cost: G&A, infrastructure, engineering/product, vendors, tax.

Do not over-show:

- Academic Support unless asked.
- Brand Marketing detail unless it is driving variance.
- Every accrual drilldown.

Decision ask:

- Which cost categories are variable with revenue?
- Which are fixed commitments?
- Which require a cap from May onward?

Transition:

> The biggest controllable operating lever is sales productivity.

### 7. Sales Team Efficiency

Purpose: Decide where to grow, freeze, or challenge headcount.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `Sales Team Efficiency`.
- Keep period at `Apr-26`.
- Show Contribution per Agent.
- Show Sales ROI.
- Show Sales Volume per Agent.
- Show Headcount.
- Then switch to `YTD`.

Say:

- This page is not about sales activity.
- It is about sales productivity.
- Hiring decisions should follow contribution per agent, not total team size.
- Dhaka and Jessore are lower ROI than CX and Retention.
- CX and Retention produce stronger contribution per agent.

Use only this grouping:

- Dhaka Telesales
- Jessore Telesales
- CX
- Retention

Do not split CX into inbound/outbound in the CEO story.

Decision ask:

- Freeze or slow low-productivity hiring.
- Shift capacity toward teams with higher contribution per agent.
- Set minimum ROI threshold.
- Set minimum contribution-per-agent threshold.

Transition:

> Next, we need to make sure acquisition spend is being measured on true cost, not just platform spend.

### 8. Marketing & Growth

Purpose: Explain whether acquisition spend is efficient.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `Marketing & Growth`.
- Show Platform Spend vs Actual Spend.
- Show ROAS trend.
- Mention adjusted ROAS, not only platform ROAS.
- Use 12M trend selector only if CEO asks for historical context.

Say:

- Platform spend understates true acquisition cost.
- Adjusted ad spend includes overpayment, FX, tax, and gateway impact.
- Adjusted ROAS is the governance metric.
- April adjusted ROAS was about 3.62x.
- This is a spend efficiency issue, not just a marketing volume issue.

Decision ask:

- Set adjusted ROAS floor.
- Separate media performance from FX and tax leakage.
- Stop scaling spend where adjusted ROAS is below threshold.

Transition:

> If he asks why adjusted spend is higher than platform spend, show USD Payment Structure. Otherwise keep moving.

### 9. USD Payment Structure

Purpose: Explain FX/payment leakage only if it affects management action.

Dashboard action:

- Go to `USD Payment Structure`.
- Show KPI cards.
- Show service waterfall.
- Avoid spending more than 2 minutes unless CEO asks.

Say:

- This page explains why platform spend and actual paid cost differ.
- April vendor bill was about $48.6K.
- All-in cost was about $64.4K.
- Extra payment over invoice was about $15.8K.
- This is not a core P&L page, but it explains marketing cost leakage.

Decision ask:

- Review payment route.
- Separate VAT, commission, and FX impact from media performance.
- Evaluate whether payment process can reduce all-in cost.

Transition:

> I will close with the decisions required from this review.

### 10. Close With Decisions

Purpose: End with action, not reporting.

Say:

> The dashboard now gives us the governance view. The decisions are: profit gap ownership, cash trigger threshold, sales hiring rules, marketing ROAS floor, and indirect cost cap.

End with these 5 asks:

1. Approve owner for the $203K profit gap bridge.
2. Set minimum cash threshold and weekly trigger review.
3. Freeze low-productivity sales hiring until contribution per agent improves.
4. Set adjusted ROAS floor.
5. Cap indirect cost run-rate unless CEO-approved.

## What To Show And What Not To Show

### Must Show

- Forecast Outlook KPI cards.
- Forecast Outlook FY bridge.
- Cash & Runway pessimistic July view.
- P&L Actual vs Forecast table.
- Revenue by channel.
- Revenue by course.
- Cost & Margins: COGS, S&M, Indirect Cost.
- Sales Team: contribution per agent and ROI.
- Marketing: adjusted ROAS and actual spend.

### Show Only If Asked

- USD Payment Structure detail.
- Accrual drilldowns.
- Every sales table row.
- Every Revenue Analysis chart.
- Same Month Previous Year comparison.
- Individual COGS sub-lines unless margin is challenged.

### Avoid Completely

- Explaining dashboard design.
- Explaining old Budget.
- Walking page by page mechanically.
- Saying "this dashboard shows a lot of information."
- Apologizing for data or structure.
- Overusing toggles.

## CEO Talk Track In One Line

Use this sequence:

1. Are we on track for $500K+ profit?
2. Where is the gap?
3. Can cash handle downside?
4. Which revenue streams can close the gap?
5. Which costs must be controlled?
6. Which teams deserve growth or freeze?
7. What decisions do we need today?

## Rapid Response If CEO Interrupts

### If he says: "Are we on track or not?"

Say:

> Not yet. FY EBITDA outlook is about $297K against the $500K+ ambition, so the gap is about $203K. We need a May-Dec operating bridge.

### If he says: "Where is the problem?"

Say:

> Gross margin is strong. The gap is mainly below gross margin: S&M productivity, brand spend, indirect cost, and cash timing.

### If he says: "Is cash okay?"

Say:

> Base case is manageable. Downside is tight around July. Pessimistic July opening cash is about $5.5K, so we need a trigger before June closes.

### If he says: "What do you need from me?"

Say:

> Five decisions: profit gap owner, cash threshold, sales hiring rule, adjusted ROAS floor, and indirect cost cap.

### If he says: "Why did you change budget?"

Say:

> For governance, actual vs forecast is more useful now. We are managing the latest full-year outlook, not comparing against a stale static budget.
